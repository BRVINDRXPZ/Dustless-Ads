# KPI Hierarchy

> **Planning specification — not connected or activated.** No live accounts, credentials, customer data, or invented business performance. Any prototype values are synthetic demonstration data.

## Purpose

Prevents vanity metrics from controlling investment.

## Level 1 — Business outcomes

Revenue; gross profit; jobs won; CAC; gross-profit return on marketing spend; repeat revenue; referral revenue.

## Level 2 — Sales outcomes

Qualified leads; scheduled estimates; completed estimates; proposals; close rate; sales cycle; pipeline value.

## Level 3 — Marketing outcomes

Leads; cost per qualified lead; calls; form submissions; organic visibility; review growth; content-assisted leads; contractor conversations.

## Level 4 — Diagnostic metrics

Clicks; CTR; CPC; impressions; views; watch time; form starts; page engagement; search-term quality; response time.

## Precedence rule

Lower levels explain higher levels; they never override them. Scale/stop decisions require reconciled Level 1 evidence plus capacity and data-quality review. When Level 1 is unavailable, label the decision **Hold—outcome data incomplete**.
