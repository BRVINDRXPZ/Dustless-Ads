# Ethical Review Generation System

## Eligibility and timing
Invite **every genuine customer in the defined completed-project cohort**, regardless of sentiment, after completion/acceptance and once immediate administrative issues are addressed—not after asking if they are happy. Contractor/commercial partner requests occur after a completed attributable engagement. Past customers require verified relationship, lawful contact basis and owner approval.

## Workflow
1. Operations marks `project_complete_review_eligible` with completion date and channel permission; unresolved experience goes to service recovery **without removing eligibility or conditioning a future invitation**.
2. Owner or designated coordinator sends one neutral request through the approved CRM/channel, ideally within a proposed 1–3 business days of completion.
3. Provide the owner-controlled direct review link as-is; validate destination quarterly and never redirect selectively.
4. One gentle reminder is the proposed maximum, 7–10 days later, only if no opt-out and no known submission. Never pressure or infer a review was not left from rating data.
5. Log request/reminder/opt-out, not review content guesses. Stop immediately on opt-out.

## CRM states
`not_eligible`, `eligible`, `request_queued`, `request_sent`, `reminder_sent`, `opted_out`, `review_observed` (public observation only), `closed`, `service_recovery_open`. Required: project/relationship ID, cohort rule, completion date, sender, channel permission, timestamps, template version, link version; no rating prediction.

## Accountability and targets
Coordinator runs weekly eligible-versus-sent exception report; owner reviews monthly. Goals are process-based: invite 100% of the owner-approved eligible cohort, zero gated/incentivized requests, zero messages after opt-out, and 100% of observed reviews triaged. Set no fabricated count goal; after a baseline, owner may adopt a capacity-appropriate count target.

Monitor monthly request volume, observed review volume, time pattern, response coverage, removals and anomalies; do not manufacture “velocity.” Proposed public response SLA: acknowledge ordinary reviews within 2 business days; complaints triaged internally within 1 business day. Owner must approve staffing and SLA.

## Non-negotiables
No incentives, gating, sentiment screening, customer-written-by-business text, review stations, fake reviews, employee/connected-party deception, pressure, or exact suggested wording presented as the customer’s voice. Optional truthful topics: service, communication, cleanliness, timeline, result, city/neighborhood only if comfortable, repair outcome, coordination.
