# Review Response Playbook

## Rules
Use the reviewer’s displayed name only if comfortable; mention one non-sensitive verified detail; thank them; stay brief. Never expose address, dates, price, health, dispute records or job specifics; diagnose publicly; argue; promise outcomes; admit fault/liability; or accuse fraud. Move resolution offline through the public business contact, not a private number. One trained reviewer drafts; owner/escalation lead approves sensitive responses.

## Frameworks (customize—never auto-post)
| Scenario | Response framework |
|---|---|
| Five-star detailed | “Thank you, [name]. We appreciate you sharing your experience with [verified general service]. The team will be glad to hear that [non-private theme] stood out.” |
| Five-star short | “Thank you for taking the time to leave a review. We appreciate the opportunity to help with your hardwood floors.” |
| Four-star | “Thank you for the thoughtful feedback. We appreciate [specific positive]. We also take your note about [general issue] seriously and will use it to improve.” |
| Neutral | “Thank you for sharing this feedback. We want to understand the experience and review our records. Please contact [public channel] so we can follow up privately.” |
| General complaint | “Thank you for raising this. We’re sorry the experience did not meet expectations. We would like to review the details privately; please contact [public channel].” |
| Scheduling | “Thank you for the feedback. Clear scheduling updates matter. We would like to review what happened and speak privately at [public channel].” |
| Dust | “Thank you for telling us. Sanding and dust expectations deserve clear communication. We will review the documented scope and would like to discuss your experience privately at [public channel].” |
| Color expectation | “Thank you for sharing this concern. Color can look different across samples, wood and lighting, and we want to review the approved selections and result privately. Please contact [public channel].” |
| Matching | “Thank you for the feedback. Matching new and existing wood can involve material and age variation. We would like to review this specific project privately at [public channel].” |
| Timeline | “Thank you for raising the timeline concern. We want to review the schedule and communications without discussing project details publicly. Please contact [public channel].” |
| Price | “Thank you for the feedback. We aim to communicate scope and pricing clearly. We would like to review the proposal and any changes privately at [public channel].” |
| Communication | “Thank you for pointing this out. Timely, clear communication matters. We would like to understand where the process fell short; please contact [public channel].” |
| Inaccurate information | “Thank you for the feedback. Our records differ on some details, but we will not discuss private project information here. Please contact [public channel] so we can compare information directly.” |
| Potential fraud | “We cannot identify this experience from the information provided. We take feedback seriously; please contact [public channel] with details so we can investigate.” Do not accuse; preserve evidence and use platform process only after validation. |
| Contractor/commercial | “Thank you for sharing your experience coordinating with our team. We appreciate the opportunity to support the verified scope and value the working relationship.” |

## Final check
Is it human, calm, specific but non-private, non-defensive, readable, accurate, and free of liability admissions? Has the correct escalation owner approved it?
