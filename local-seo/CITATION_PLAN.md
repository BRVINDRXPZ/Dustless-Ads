# Citation Plan

## Purpose and selection
Prioritize accurate entity data and genuine referral/industry relevance—not listing count. A candidate qualifies only if legitimate, maintained, discoverable to the intended audience, compatible with the real business model and safe for the approved address policy.

## Workflow
1. Lock `NAP_STANDARD.md`; verify legal/public name, address visibility, phone, URL, hours, categories and credentials.
2. Search each platform and the web for existing/duplicate records; capture URL, observed NAP, owner/access, evidence date and conflicts.
3. Validate official ownership, cost, membership, category, address-display, editing/removal and current terms. Reject scraped/spam networks.
4. Prioritize corrections to authoritative inconsistencies before net-new listings.
5. Prepare exact change/submission package and proof; owner approves platform-by-platform. This phase does not submit.
6. Authorized executor records status/date/login owner (never credentials), public URL and annual recheck.

## Tiers
Tier 1: verified core ecosystems/data providers and major consumer platforms with meaningful NAP reach. Tier 2: legitimate local chambers/associations, construction/design/historic/hospitality organizations, suppliers/manufacturers where qualification is real. Tier 3: commercial vendor systems required by actual buyers. Reject low-quality directories, forced reciprocal links, hidden renewals, false categories or lead resale incompatible with owner policy.

## Audit metrics
Approved NAP match rate; duplicate/conflict count; priority corrections completed; owner-controlled records; referral/qualified-lead evidence. Do not claim link equity or rankings from a listing.
