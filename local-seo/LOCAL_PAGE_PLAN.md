# Local Page Plan

## Decision rule
A page needs distinct intent, operational relevance, unique local expertise, real proof potential, a defined internal-link role and conversion purpose, and enough differentiation to avoid doorway/thin content. A neighborhood name alone fails. Scores in `LOCAL_PAGE_SCORECARD.csv` are planning judgments, not keyword-volume findings.

## Core service architecture
- Build first after proof: refinishing, dust-controlled sanding (qualified claims), installation, repair/restoration.
- Evidence-gated: historic/heart-pine, commercial, contractor partnerships.
- Consolidate initially: stairs and screen-and-coat as robust sections or guides until intent/proof support standalone pages.
- Every service page: eligibility, process, limitations, FAQs, general service-area context, permissioned proof, related services, estimate CTA, schema only when visible/supported.

## Geographic architecture
- New Orleans hub: useful citywide context and links to services; not a duplicate service page.
- Metairie: candidate after operations/proof confirmation.
- Uptown/Garden District, Lakeview/Mid-City, Marigny/Bywater: cluster or case-study pathways first; split only with distinct projects/questions/material context.
- Kenner, Mandeville, Covington, Slidell and Northshore: evidence-gated. Avoid city pages competing with a generic Northshore page.
- No River Ridge, Harahan, Madisonville, Westbank or LA/MS standalone page until distinct demand, operations and proof justify it.

## Page brief requirements
Primary query intent; audience; scope/exclusions; approved geography; expert input; two or more substantively unique local sections; proof IDs/permissions; CTA; internal links; canonical/title/H1; map/address privacy; NAP; measurement events; last-reviewed date; owner and technical approval.

## Cannibalization control
Map one primary intent to one canonical page. Search Console later tests query overlap; consolidate/redirect only after evidence and authorization. Do not swap city names across cloned templates. No publication under Phase 6.
