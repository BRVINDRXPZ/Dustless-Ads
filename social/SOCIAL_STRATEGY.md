# Phase 7 Social Strategy

> **Status:** Internal production system—drafts only. Nothing here authorizes publishing, account connection, outreach, spend, or use of an asset without recorded permission and claim review.

## Operating guardrails
- Use real, accurately labeled project media only. AI/synthetic imagery is never project proof.
- A project, customer, testimonial, precise location, property exterior, crew member, product claim, credential, result, schedule, or price appears only after its specific fact and permission fields are cleared.
- Say “dust-controlled” only after process wording is approved; never promise dust-free conditions.
- Move addresses, scope details, availability, budgets, complaints, and customer information to the approved private lead path.
- Draft, review, and schedule are separate states. Only the owner-designated publisher may publish after final approval.

## Strategic job
Build memory before demand and confidence at decision time: recognizable craft footage, honest assessment, local property literacy, controlled process, and scope clarity. Social supports—rather than replaces—Search, Maps, project proof, contractor development, and measured lead handling.

## Audience-to-outcome map
| Audience | Need | Content job | Conversion |
|---|---|---|---|
| Homeowner | Know whether to restore, repair, refinish, or install | Show diagnosis, process, limitations, matched proof | Approved estimate form/call |
| Historic owner | Material-sensitive judgment | Verified education plus permissioned cases | Assessment request |

## Recognizable content grammar
1. Open on wood, damage, or finished detail—not a logo.
2. Cover uses warm wood texture, charcoal/cream type, 3–6 word series title, small series tab; final colors/fonts require brand approval.
3. First spoken sentence identifies the decision or constraint. Natural jobsite sound precedes music where useful.
4. Show condition → assessment → controlled process → outcome → limitation/next decision.
5. Close with one CTA. No generic motivation, unsupported superlatives, or manufactured urgency.

## Production model: one qualified project → an asset stack
Capture once; produce one 45–90s hero, 2–4 shorts, one matched before/after carousel, one explanation, one ASMR cut, one contractor lesson where relevant, one GBP-ready post, 3–6 Stories, gallery selects, case-study notes, and clean ad-ready variants. Every derivative inherits the source permission and claim restrictions.

## Cadence proposal (validate against capacity)
Three original ideas weekly: one proof/reveal, one explanation, one lower-lift process/field note. Repurpose selectively across short-form surfaces; add Stories from cleared assets; one GBP derivative weekly only when locally useful. LinkedIn receives B2B material, not every homeowner Reel. Long-form is monthly at most and only when a complete, evidenced story warrants it.

## Funnel and measurement
Awareness: qualified reach, 3-second views, audience mix. Consideration: watch time, completion, saves, shares, profile visits. Decision: website clicks, calls, estimate/contractor/commercial inquiries. Business: qualified leads, assisted leads, wins/value influence when CRM evidence exists. Never optimize solely for views.

## Governance
Capture lead records rights → editor creates draft → fact owner verifies → privacy/claims reviewer clears → owner approves copy/CTA/timing → publisher runs preflight → metrics owner records outcome. A withdrawal freezes all derivatives. See approval workflow and asset register.

## 90-day outcomes (process, not forecasts)
- Establish capture compliance, consistent franchise grammar, and asset lineage.
- Learn which creative patterns attract qualified homeowner and B2B attention.
- Build a permissioned proof library and GBP/local-search derivatives.
- Produce clean masters suitable for later paid testing without launching ads.

## Ruthless content priorities
### Launch First
- **Finish Friday:** matched reveal Reel/Short plus Instagram/Facebook carousel and GBP derivative.
- **The Floor Guys Explain:** 20–45s owner/crew voiceover on Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, and YouTube Shorts.
- **Can These Floors Be Saved?:** assessment-first short only when a real project is cleared.
- **Floor ASMR:** 8–20s natural-sound cuts as low-burden support, never unsafe instruction.
- **Contractor Field Notes:** one concise LinkedIn post/video every other week.

### Build Next
Floor Rescue heroes, project case-study packages, What Would This Cost? variable explainers, NOLA Floor Report, systematic Stories, creative experiments, long-form project episodes, and approved paid-social variant banks.

### Hold Until Proof Exists
New Orleans Floor History claims; heart-pine/species identification; named historic, hotel, restaurant, church, contractor, or commercial cases; testimonials; customer reactions; sensitive project names/locations; credentials; exact product/durability/cure claims; scheduling windows; and “The Floor Guys Contractors Call” campaign use.

### Do Not Post
Fake project proof; customer information without permission; competitor attacks; dangerous instructions; misleading cost claims; unsupported superiority; fake urgency; review manipulation; random trend chasing; generic filler; content that damages premium positioning; or content designed mainly for unqualified DIY/tool-rental traffic.
