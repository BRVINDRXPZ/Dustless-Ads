# Content Pillars

> **Status:** Internal production system—drafts only. Nothing here authorizes publishing, account connection, outreach, spend, or use of an asset without recorded permission and claim review.

## Operating guardrails
- Use real, accurately labeled project media only. AI/synthetic imagery is never project proof.
- A project, customer, testimonial, precise location, property exterior, crew member, product claim, credential, result, schedule, or price appears only after its specific fact and permission fields are cleared.
- Say “dust-controlled” only after process wording is approved; never promise dust-free conditions.
- Move addresses, scope details, availability, budgets, complaints, and customer information to the approved private lead path.
- Draft, review, and schedule are separate states. Only the owner-designated publisher may publish after final approval.

| Pillar | Audience/job | Evidence | Core franchises | Mix proposal | Guardrail |
|---|---|---|---|---:|---|
| Transformation proof | Make capability visible | Matched real before/during/after; scope facts | Floor Rescue; Finish Friday | 30% | No outcome implication beyond shown scope |
| Decision clarity | Help buyers choose next step | Approved technical process | Floor Guys Explain; Saved?; Restore It | 25% | Assessment, not remote diagnosis |
| Craft/process | Make work recognizable and satisfying | Safe real process footage | Floor ASMR; Floor Crimes | 15% | No dangerous tutorial or humiliation |
| Local material literacy | Connect expertise to place | Expert-reviewed sources and verified species | NOLA Floor History; Floor Report | 10% | Hold factual history until sourced |
| Trade/commercial readiness | Build B2B confidence | Documents, cases, actual operating practices | Field Notes; Contractors Call | 15% | Campaign concept is not factual claim |
| People/brand | Human trust without filler | Crew consent; actual roles | Floor Report; behind scenes | 5% | No private data or staged fake work |

Apply the mix over a month, not every week. Cost-factor content belongs to decision clarity and discusses variables, never bait pricing.
