# Phase 1 Executive Action Plan

**Status:** Proposed foundation for owner review

**Prepared:** 2026-07-20

**Scope guardrail:** This plan does not include competitor research, advertisements, landing-page production, publishing, outreach, spending, or changes to live accounts.

## Mission

Build NOLA Dustless Hardwood Floors into a highly visible, recognizable, trusted, and frequently chosen hardwood-flooring specialist across Greater New Orleans and approved Northshore markets. Growth should favor profitable, operationally suitable work and be supported by verifiable proof, fast lead handling, disciplined follow-up, and end-to-end measurement.

## Business Objectives

1. Capture high-intent demand for priority hardwood services before investing in broad awareness.
2. Increase the flow of qualified opportunities for whole-home refinishing, installation, historic restoration, and other owner-confirmed profitable scopes.
3. Establish separate, repeatable acquisition paths for homeowners, contractors, and commercial or hospitality buyers.
4. Build a recognizable specialist brand using only verified claims and approved real-project evidence.
5. Connect marketing activity to qualified leads, estimates, proposals, wins, revenue, and gross profit.
6. Match lead volume and targeting to estimating, communication, and crew capacity.

## Initial Competitive Hypothesis

> **Unvalidated hypothesis—not competitor research or a finding.**

NOLA Dustless may be able to create an advantage by presenting itself consistently as a local hardwood specialist that combines restoration judgment, dust-controlled sanding, historic-property awareness, modern project documentation, and residential-to-commercial capability. A focused message, strong verified project proof, disciplined response and follow-up, and cleaner attribution may outperform less specialized or less systematic alternatives.

This hypothesis must be tested in Phase 2 through structured market and competitor research. No comparative or superiority claim should be published unless supportable.

## Risks

| Risk | Consequence | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Demand exceeds response or field capacity | Slow responses, weak customer experience, operational strain | Use capacity gates, staged rollout, scheduling feedback, and pause rules |
| Targeting attracts poor-fit jobs | Wasted estimating time and lower profitability | Confirm scope, geography, and minimums; add qualification criteria and negative targeting later |
| Claims or proof lack verification or permission | Trust, legal, and reputational exposure | Maintain a proof register and approval gate before public use |
| Incomplete attribution | Spend decisions based on clicks or raw leads | Define lifecycle and source rules before launch; QA tracking |
| Access or ownership gaps | Delayed audits and unreliable baselines | Create an access inventory; use least-privilege access; never store secrets in this repository |
| Premature channel expansion | Diluted effort and weak learning | Follow the priority stack and phase exit criteria |
| Overreliance on a single channel | Volatile opportunity flow | Establish search/local foundations first, then diversify deliberately |
| Unverified positioning | Generic or misleading messaging | Complete Phase 2 research and secure owner approval before publication |

## Initial Success Metrics

No performance baseline or numeric target is asserted in Phase 1. Baselines must be measured from approved sources, and targets must be set after the owner confirms economics and capacity.

### Primary business outcomes

- **Qualified leads:** inquiries meeting approved service, geography, timing, and project-size criteria.
- **Estimates scheduled and completed:** count and rate from qualified leads.
- **Proposals issued:** count, value where available, and rate from completed estimates.
- **Wins:** count, booked revenue, and win rate from proposals.
- **Gross profit:** total and by service or source where reliable cost data exists.
- **Revenue attribution coverage:** share of won work with a documented source.

### Operating and diagnostic measures

- Median first-response time during approved lead-handling hours.
- Contact rate and lead-to-estimate rate.
- Disqualification volume and reasons.
- Estimate-to-proposal and proposal-to-win rates.
- Cost per qualified lead and cost per win after paid activity is explicitly approved and launched.
- Service-area and service-category mix.
- Tracking QA pass rate and missing-source rate.
- Google Business Profile actions, organic visibility, paid search activity, and website conversion rate only as diagnostic measures—not standalone definitions of success.

### Baseline protocol

For every metric, record the definition, system of record, owner, reporting frequency, available historical window, current baseline, approved target, and known limitations. Use `TBD—owner input` or `TBD—baseline required` rather than estimates presented as facts.

## Ruthless Priority Stack

The rank below is strict. It reflects the combined criteria of revenue potential, implementation speed, defensible advantage, measurement quality, and operational feasibility—not enthusiasm for a channel.

1. **Confirm profitable work, service geography, project minimums, capacity, and lead acceptance rules.** Prevents growth from amplifying unprofitable or unserviceable demand.
2. **Define the lead-to-revenue lifecycle and system of record.** Makes every later acquisition decision measurable against business outcomes.
3. **Inventory access, baselines, assets, and proof permissions.** Unlocks reliable audits while preventing unsafe or unauthorized use.
4. **Audit the current high-intent customer journey and tracking.** Identifies friction and measurement failures before traffic is increased; audit only after access approval.
5. **Complete structured market and competitor research.** Validates demand, gaps, positioning, and channel priorities before messaging or build work.
6. **Approve positioning and a proof-backed message hierarchy.** Converts real strengths into a consistent advantage without unsupported claims.
7. **Specify conversion tracking, qualification, attribution, and QA.** Creates launch-quality measurement; implementation requires explicit access approval.
8. **Strengthen Google Search, Maps, and website readiness.** Addresses the highest-intent discovery surfaces before broad awareness; live modifications require approval.
9. **Prepare tightly scoped paid-search and conversion plans.** Targets approved, profitable demand with budget and capacity controls; no ads or pages are created in Phase 1.
10. **Systematize response, estimate follow-up, and nurture.** Improves conversion of demand already generated without relying solely on more traffic.
11. **Build contractor and commercial partner pipelines.** Creates differentiated repeat and higher-value opportunity sources once targeting and capacity are clear.
12. **Scale real-project content, social familiarity, partnerships, PR, and experiments.** Broadens recognition only after proof, operations, and measurement can support it.
