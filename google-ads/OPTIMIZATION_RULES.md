# Optimization Rules

## Decision hierarchy

Optimize in this order: tracking integrity → service/geography fit → qualified lead → estimate → proposal/win → revenue/gross profit → volume. CTR and ad strength are diagnostics, not business outcomes.

## Rules

- Pause only for clear safety/quality failures or after a predeclared evidence window accounting for conversion lag; do not use arbitrary click counts as universal truth.
- Increase budgets in measured steps only when marginal qualified economics, impression-share opportunity, and capacity all support it.
- Reduce budgets before loosening qualification when operations are constrained.
- Do not change bid strategy because the UI recommends it; document expected mechanism and rollback.
- Add broad match only in an isolated later test with qualified Smart Bidding signals and daily query review.
- Keep Brand reporting separate; do not use inexpensive brand conversions to claim nonbrand acquisition success.
- Review device, hour, network, geography, and audience observations for lead quality; apply exclusions/adjustments only where current bidding controls permit and evidence supports them.
- Replace disapproved or misleading copy; never work around policy review.
- Use one major experimental variable at a time and retain losers long enough to evaluate down-funnel quality when safe.
- Reconcile conversion totals and values weekly; suspend automation if primary data becomes corrupted.

## Kill criteria

Kill or rebuild a campaign when, across an owner-approved evidence window: demand is predominantly wrong-service/unserviceable; qualified CAC exceeds the economic ceiling without a credible fix; landing intent cannot be matched; claims/proof cannot be approved; operational capacity cannot support it; or measurement cannot distinguish outcomes. Sparse demand alone moves a campaign to hold; it does not prove no market exists.
