# Search-Term Review Protocol

## Cadence

- Days 1–14 after authorized launch: every business day; emergency review after spend anomaly.
- Weeks 3–8: at least three times weekly.
- Mature campaigns: weekly, plus after expansion, algorithm, geo, match-type, or offer changes.

## Review fields

Search term, keyword, match type, campaign/ad group, user/matched location, cost, primary conversions, qualified status, disqualification reason, estimate/win/value if mature, intent class, action, reviewer, date, evidence window.

## Actions

1. **Keep:** relevant and appropriately routed.
2. **Promote:** add high-value recurring query as exact/phrase in the best ad group; do not duplicate needlessly.
3. **Negative:** clear irrelevant intent. Choose account, shared-list, campaign, or ad-group scope; use the least destructive match.
4. **Route:** add campaign negative and positive keyword elsewhere only when audience/service/geo and landing page differ.
5. **Investigate:** ambiguous term without enough context; inspect call/form disposition before blocking.

## Negative safeguards

Never negative a term merely because it has no conversion in a tiny sample. Check close variants and semantic reach. Avoid broad single-word negatives such as `repair`, `wood`, `commercial`, `pine`, `water`, or town names. Employment, DIY, rentals, tools, products, and wrong-floor categories may be blocked when intent is clear. Log every negative with rationale and rollback owner.

## Escalation

Immediately pause affected scope for systemic tracking failure, policy/claim risk, wrong country/state, uncontrolled irrelevant spending, fraudulent patterns, or capacity shutdown. Owner approval is required before new geography, service, offer, or material budget expansion.
