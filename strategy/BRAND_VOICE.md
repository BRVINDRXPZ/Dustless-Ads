# Brand Voice

**Personality:** skilled, local, modern, responsive, trustworthy, premium but approachable, and commercially disciplined.

## Voice rules

| Element | Rule |
|---|---|
| Tone | Calm, direct, useful, candid. Never arrogant, corny, desperate, fake-luxury, or bureaucratic. |
| Vocabulary | Concrete nouns and verbs: assess, restore, repair, sand, install, scope, sample, coordinate, document. |
| Sentence style | Short-to-medium sentences; active voice; one idea at a time; bullets for requirements. |
| Confidence | Be decisive about process, conditional about unknown conditions and outcomes. |
| Local references | Use exact verified places/property context naturally; avoid clichés, caricature, and claiming all NOLA homes are alike. |
| Humor | Light and rare; never about damage, cost, safety, customers, neighborhoods, or competitors. No forced puns. |

## Tone by context

- **Homeowner:** reassuring without minimizing disruption; translate technical choices.
- **Contractor:** concise, scope-first, schedule-aware; state inputs, exclusions, dependencies, and next action.
- **Commercial:** procurement-ready, risk-aware, factual; address access, phasing, operating constraints, documents, and closeout.
- **Social:** visual and educational; explain the decision, not just “look at this transformation.”
- **Sales:** diagnose before persuading; compare scopes rather than attacking price.
- **Review response:** grateful and specific only to public/approved facts; never reveal scope, address, dispute details, or private data.

## Lexicon

**Use:** hardwood specialist (after owner approval); dust-controlled (after process verification); condition-led; preserve where feasible; written scope; clear expectations; project fit; sample; verified; phased plan; before/process/after; Greater New Orleans (after territory confirmation).

**Avoid:** dust-free; miracle; flawless; exact match; luxury for less; game-changing; best/#1/only; cheap; guys who do it all; no mess; no odor; instant; guaranteed; “historic” or species labels guessed from images.

## Examples (drafts; proof-gated)

### Before-and-after captions

1. “The decision came before the sanding. We assessed the worn areas, agreed on the scope, and documented the process from the same angle. Final project facts and image permission required before posting.”
2. “Old character did not need to mean an unclear plan. This floor was evaluated for repair and refinishing, with visible limitations discussed before work began. Add only verified material and finish details.”
3. “Commercial result, operational plan: condition, access, sequence, finish restrictions, and reopening requirements were reviewed before mobilization. Publish only with project permission.”

### Ad-copy examples (examples only; do not create or publish ads)

- “Can your hardwood be restored? Start with a floor assessment and a clear scope. Send project details.”
- “Hardwood refinishing with a controlled, clearly explained process. Ask about project fit.”
- “Contractor hardwood scope? Submit plans, location, target schedule, and site conditions for review.”

### Landing-page examples (copy examples only; do not build pages)

- **Headline:** “Understand your floor before deciding what comes next.” **Support:** “We assess condition, goals, repair needs, and project constraints before recommending restoration, refinishing, or installation.”
- **Commercial:** “A hardwood scope planned around the property—not just the finish.” **Support:** “Start with bid inputs, access, phasing, use, and closeout requirements.”

### Contractor outreach examples (do not send)

“NOLA Dustless is developing a structured intake for qualified hardwood scopes. A complete request would include plans/square footage, site address, existing conditions, specified material/finish, target schedule, and trade dependencies. Capability, territory, and response terms must be owner-approved before use.”

### Review responses

- Positive: “Thank you for trusting our team with your hardwood project. We appreciate you taking the time to share your experience.”
- Concern: “Thank you for raising this. We want to review the documented scope and conditions directly. Please contact us through our official channel so we can discuss the project privately.”
