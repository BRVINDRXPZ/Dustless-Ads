# Claims and Proof Policy

## Publication gate

No claim is approved merely because it appears in this repository. Before use, record: exact wording, source, evidence owner, date checked, applicable service/geography, asset/customer permission, expiration/recheck date, and approver. Marketing must make a narrower claim when evidence is narrower. A competitor's self-description is never proof about NOLA Dustless.

## Classes

| Class | Meaning | Examples / action |
|---|---|---|
| Safe factual claims | Stable, directly controlled facts after final accuracy check | Company name; official contact path; a non-claim CTA. Avoid implying current availability. |
| Owner-verification required | Operational facts the owner must define and keep current | Current services, territory, scheduling, minimums, pricing policy, process, crew/capacity. |
| Evidence required | Objective or performance statements needing current records | License/insurance, equipment/process, years, project history, commercial capability, durability. |
| Permission required | Accurate facts/assets involving another party or protected media | Customer/project names, addresses, review quotes, photos/video, logos, architect/GC relationship. |
| High-risk claims | Legal, comparative, absolute, health/safety, warranty, or consequential promises | Dustless as an absolute, best/#1/only, guaranteed result, health/environment claims, deadlines. Legal review may be appropriate. |
| Prohibited claims | Do not use under this strategy | Dust-free/zero dust; fabricated/altered reviews; invented projects/statistics; unsupported superlatives; exact/invisible match guarantees; false urgency/scarcity. |

## Claim-specific rules

| Claim/topic | Class | Minimum substantiation and allowed direction |
|---|---|---|
| Licensed and insured | Evidence required | Current license record/classification and policy/COI; owner/legal approval of exact wording and geography. Never imply work outside classification/coverage. |
| Dustless | High risk / brand-name context | Company name may be used accurately; a process claim needs equipment/workflow evidence and limitations. Prefer approved “dust-controlled.” |
| Dust-free | Prohibited absent extraordinary legal substantiation | Strategy prohibits it; normal residual dust makes absolutes risky. |
| Best / number one / only | High risk; not recommended | Avoid. A defined, current, independent substantiation basis would be required. |
| Historic-floor specialist | Evidence required | Multiple verified relevant cases, methods/material competence, permission, and owner-approved scope; otherwise say “historic-floor feasibility assessment” only as an internal proposed offer. |
| Commercial experience | Evidence + permission | Verified scopes, dates, role, relevant capacity and documents; name projects/customers only with written permission. |
| Project/customer names | Permission required | Written approval defining name, logo, location/detail, channel, duration. |
| Review quotes | Permission/policy required | Exact text, source/link/date, identity display rules, platform terms, no selective edits that change meaning. |
| Before-and-after images | Permission + accuracy | Rights, same project, truthful labels, no deceptive edits, dates/context, removal process; disclose illustrative limits where needed. |
| Years of experience | Evidence required | Define whose/what experience and calculate from records; avoid aggregating crew years misleadingly. |
| Service area | Owner verification | Exact currently serviceable places and exclusions; Northshore minimum/travel rules; do not confuse willingness with regular coverage. |
| Guarantees | High risk | Written terms, responsible entity, remedy, exclusions, duration, operational/legal approval; never improvise in copy. |
| Pricing/free offers | Owner verification | Current scope, taxes/fees, conditions, expiration, and capacity. Do not publish ranges until economics and policy are approved. |
| Scheduling/deadlines | Owner verification + project basis | State estimates and dependencies; product instructions govern return-to-use. No immediate/same-day promise without capacity evidence. |
| Finish durability | Evidence required | Exact product/system, preparation, use, care, manufacturer technical data, and no extrapolated lifespan. |
| Environmental/health/safety | High risk | Product SDS/technical documents and qualified review; never infer “safe,” “non-toxic,” “green,” allergy/asthma benefit, indoor-air benefit, or child/pet safety. |
| Color/species/match | Evidence required | Samples and competent identification; explain variability. Never guarantee exact stain or invisible board match. |

## Proof ladder

1. **Level 0 — assertion:** not publishable as a capability claim.
2. **Level 1 — owner confirmation:** usable only for current operational facts after approval.
3. **Level 2 — record:** license, policy, product data, contract/scope, dated project fact sheet.
4. **Level 3 — permissioned demonstration:** matched media, case narrative, outcome and limitations.
5. **Level 4 — corroboration:** permissioned review/reference or independent source relevant to the exact claim.

Comparative, historic-authority, and material performance claims generally need Levels 2–4. Recheck volatile claims before each campaign and remove expired proof.

## Image and copy controls

- Keep originals; record editor, crop/color corrections, and AI use. Do not generate or materially alter “proof.”
- Separate public facts from internal notes. Never expose addresses, customer data, documents, or access credentials.
- Route corrections and permission withdrawals promptly across all known uses.
- When uncertain, use narrower language or do not publish.
