# Segment Personas

**Status:** planning hypotheses, not customer research or demographic findings. Validate through permissioned interviews, inquiry data, and won/lost analysis. “Best channel” is a test priority, not measured performance.

## Residential refinishing customers

- **Buyer profile:** Owner/decision-maker with existing hardwood
- **Trigger event:** Visible wear, move, renovation, or finish failure
- **Main concerns:** Dust, trust, disruption, cost, color
- **Emotional stakes:** Pride in the home; fear of irreversible damage
- **Practical stakes:** Furniture, occupancy, schedule, scope, cure
- **Questions they ask:** Can it be saved? How dusty? How long? What changes price?
- **Objections:** Higher estimate; moving out; color uncertainty
- **Trust signals:** Written scope, verified process, matched proof, permissioned reviews
- **Best offer:** Professional floor assessment
- **Best proof:** Comparable refinishing case with limitations
- **Preferred CTA:** Request a floor assessment
- **Best channel:** Search/Maps; referral; visual social
- **Likely disqualifiers:** Wrong material; below minimum; unsafe/unworkable conditions; cheapest-only
- **Estimated commercial value:** High

## Historic-property owners

- **Buyer profile:** Steward of an older or character property
- **Trigger event:** Purchase, renovation, damage, worn original floor
- **Main concerns:** Loss of character, thin boards, patches, species/source
- **Emotional stakes:** Responsibility for something irreplaceable
- **Practical stakes:** Feasibility, matching, substrate, approvals, budget
- **Questions they ask:** Can original material remain? What is it? Can patches blend?
- **Objections:** Fear of over-sanding; specialist-claim skepticism
- **Trust signals:** Verified historic cases, material reasoning, candid limits
- **Best offer:** Historic-floor feasibility assessment
- **Best proof:** Permissioned condition-to-outcome case and identification basis
- **Preferred CTA:** Request a feasibility assessment
- **Best channel:** Organic search, preservation/referral partners, visual case studies
- **Likely disqualifiers:** Demand for perfect invisibility; prohibited treatment; no verification access
- **Estimated commercial value:** Strategic

## Hardwood installation customers

- **Buyer profile:** Owner planning new or replacement hardwood
- **Trigger event:** Build, addition, failed floor, redesign
- **Main concerns:** Material fit, subfloor, transitions, finish, coordination
- **Emotional stakes:** Confidence in a lasting design choice
- **Practical stakes:** Method, moisture/substrate, schedule, other trades
- **Questions they ask:** Which material/method? Who prepares subfloor? How will rooms connect?
- **Objections:** Price versus commodity install; timeline
- **Trust signals:** Detailed scope, samples, prerequisite checklist, installation cases
- **Best offer:** Whole-home flooring plan
- **Best proof:** Verified installation and transition proof
- **Preferred CTA:** Plan an installation review
- **Best channel:** Search, designers/builders, referral
- **Likely disqualifiers:** Unsupported material choice; unready substrate; incompatible deadline
- **Estimated commercial value:** High

## Contractors and remodelers

- **Buyer profile:** GC/PM needing a flooring subcontractor
- **Trigger event:** Bid request, renovation schedule, incumbent gap
- **Main concerns:** Responsiveness, exclusions, change conditions, punch
- **Emotional stakes:** Reputation with client and trades
- **Practical stakes:** Price completeness, schedule, paperwork, crew coordination
- **Questions they ask:** What inputs? What is excluded? Can you coordinate?
- **Objections:** Unknown reliability; price; availability
- **Trust signals:** Complete bid checklist, COI/license if verified, references, issue logs
- **Best offer:** Contractor plan review
- **Best proof:** Trade reference and documented scope-to-closeout case
- **Preferred CTA:** Submit plans and scope
- **Best channel:** Direct/referral, search, LinkedIn (no outreach without approval)
- **Likely disqualifiers:** Incomplete scope; unrealistic schedule; payment/contract risk
- **Estimated commercial value:** Strategic

## Builders and developers

- **Buyer profile:** Builder, developer, superintendent, purchasing lead
- **Trigger event:** New phase, spec decision, subcontractor sourcing
- **Main concerns:** Repeatability, capacity, schedule, cost control
- **Emotional stakes:** Protecting delivery and buyer expectations
- **Practical stakes:** Volume, sequencing, standards, change control
- **Questions they ask:** Can the scope repeat? Capacity? Who owns prerequisites?
- **Objections:** Scaling risk; procurement readiness
- **Trust signals:** Verified capacity, submittals, schedule process, comparable work
- **Best offer:** Contractor plan review
- **Best proof:** Repeatable installation case and references
- **Preferred CTA:** Submit project package
- **Best channel:** Relationships, bid networks, LinkedIn
- **Likely disqualifiers:** Capacity mismatch; unclear authority; unacceptable terms
- **Estimated commercial value:** Strategic

## Architects and interior designers

- **Buyer profile:** Specifier/adviser protecting design intent
- **Trigger event:** Specification, renovation, material issue
- **Main concerns:** Feasibility, color, details, samples, substitutions
- **Emotional stakes:** Design credibility and preservation of intent
- **Practical stakes:** Approvals, lead times, constructability, documentation
- **Questions they ask:** Can this detail work? What varies? Can we sample?
- **Objections:** Aesthetic uncertainty; contractor communication
- **Trust signals:** Samples, technical data, documented constraints, precise cases
- **Best offer:** Color and finish consultation
- **Best proof:** Approved sample/process and design-relevant case
- **Preferred CTA:** Schedule a scope and sample review
- **Best channel:** Professional referral, portfolio, LinkedIn
- **Likely disqualifiers:** Demand for photo-only color guarantee; unspecified authority
- **Estimated commercial value:** Strategic

## Realtors

- **Buyer profile:** Agent needing transaction or listing clarity
- **Trigger event:** Inspection, pre-list work, buyer concern
- **Main concerns:** Speed, appearance, uncertainty, closing timing
- **Emotional stakes:** Protecting trust and deal momentum
- **Practical stakes:** Initial feasibility, scope, access, deadline
- **Questions they ask:** Repair, refinish, or disclose? Can you assess quickly?
- **Objections:** Timing and small scope
- **Trust signals:** Clear initial triage, scope caveats, permissioned transformations
- **Best offer:** Photo-based initial project review
- **Best proof:** Comparable verified case, honest response process
- **Preferred CTA:** Send photos and timeline
- **Best channel:** Referral, local search, email only with approval
- **Likely disqualifiers:** Guarantee demanded from photos; deadline impossible; tiny job
- **Estimated commercial value:** Medium

## Property managers

- **Buyer profile:** Manager balancing owner, tenant, and asset
- **Trigger event:** Turnover, complaint, damage, planned maintenance
- **Main concerns:** Access, tenants, durable care, approvals, cost
- **Emotional stakes:** Avoiding disruption and owner escalation
- **Practical stakes:** Phasing, notices, cure, repeat maintenance
- **Questions they ask:** Can units stay occupied? What must tenants do?
- **Objections:** Downtime; budget approval; vendor compliance
- **Trust signals:** Process checklist, scope, care plan, verified vendor documents
- **Best offer:** Floor condition and scope review
- **Best proof:** Occupied-site or turnover case, if verified
- **Preferred CTA:** Request a site review
- **Best channel:** Referral, search, property networks
- **Likely disqualifiers:** No site control; unsafe occupancy; approval ambiguity
- **Estimated commercial value:** High

## Hotels and hospitality groups

- **Buyer profile:** Facilities, operations, ownership, designer or procurement
- **Trigger event:** Renovation, wear, event/season window
- **Main concerns:** Guest impact, phasing, odor/dust, reopening, appearance
- **Emotional stakes:** Brand experience and revenue disruption
- **Practical stakes:** Night work, access, cure, noise, safety, closeout
- **Questions they ask:** How will operations continue? Can phases reopen?
- **Objections:** Unproven capacity; deadline; compliance
- **Trust signals:** Relevant case, phasing plan, product data, documents
- **Best offer:** Commercial site walkthrough
- **Best proof:** Permissioned hospitality case and operating plan
- **Preferred CTA:** Submit property constraints
- **Best channel:** Relationships, search, LinkedIn, procurement
- **Likely disqualifiers:** Non-negotiable unsafe deadline; unsupported night/phasing capacity
- **Estimated commercial value:** Strategic

## Restaurants

- **Buyer profile:** Operator, facilities lead, designer, or GC
- **Trigger event:** Closure window, wear, remodel, damage
- **Main concerns:** Downtime, heavy use, odor, cleanability
- **Emotional stakes:** Avoid lost service and poor guest appearance
- **Practical stakes:** Cure, access, maintenance, kitchen adjacency
- **Questions they ask:** What can fit the closure? Which system fits use?
- **Objections:** Cannot close; durability guarantee sought
- **Trust signals:** Manufacturer data, schedule assumptions, comparable case
- **Best offer:** Project phasing consultation
- **Best proof:** Verified use/finish case and care plan
- **Preferred CTA:** Request an operations-aware walkthrough
- **Best channel:** GC/referral, search, hospitality network
- **Likely disqualifiers:** Deadline conflicts with product instructions; unsuitable environment
- **Estimated commercial value:** High

## Churches and institutions

- **Buyer profile:** Facilities committee, administrator, trustee, GC
- **Trigger event:** Capital project, wear, damage, event calendar
- **Main concerns:** Stewardship, approvals, disruption, budget
- **Emotional stakes:** Duty to community and historic character
- **Practical stakes:** Phases, accessibility, meetings/events, procurement
- **Questions they ask:** Can work phase around use? Can original floors remain?
- **Objections:** Committee timing; funding; lowest bid
- **Trust signals:** Clear options, meeting-ready scope, verified institutional proof
- **Best offer:** Repair and restoration assessment
- **Best proof:** Relevant case, credentials, option comparison
- **Preferred CTA:** Schedule a site and calendar review
- **Best channel:** Referral, search, institutional networks
- **Likely disqualifiers:** No decision authority; unsafe schedule; payment uncertainty
- **Estimated commercial value:** High

## Northshore customers

- **Buyer profile:** Homeowner or trade buyer in approved secondary territory
- **Trigger event:** Qualified hardwood need not served locally
- **Main concerns:** Travel charges, availability, proof of coverage
- **Emotional stakes:** Confidence they are not an afterthought
- **Practical stakes:** Minimum scope, mobilization, schedule, service town
- **Questions they ask:** Do you serve my town? Is there a minimum?
- **Objections:** Travel and scheduling friction
- **Trust signals:** Exact approved territory/rules and local proof
- **Best offer:** Photo-based initial project review
- **Best proof:** Verified Northshore project and service rules
- **Preferred CTA:** Send location, photos, and scope
- **Best channel:** Local search/Maps after validation, referrals
- **Likely disqualifiers:** Outside territory; below travel minimum; urgent small repair
- **Estimated commercial value:** Medium

## Commercial flooring decision-makers

- **Buyer profile:** Facilities, procurement, ownership, GC, or asset manager
- **Trigger event:** Bid, capital plan, failure, tenant improvement
- **Main concerns:** Risk, documentation, scope, schedule, accountability
- **Emotional stakes:** Professional credibility and operating continuity
- **Practical stakes:** Compliance, access, phasing, closeout, cost comparability
- **Questions they ask:** Can you qualify, document, coordinate, and close out?
- **Objections:** Capability and scale skepticism; competing bids
- **Trust signals:** Bid intake, verified documents/references, phasing and issue process
- **Best offer:** Commercial bid intake process
- **Best proof:** Comparable verified project and closeout artifacts
- **Preferred CTA:** Submit bid requirements
- **Best channel:** Procurement/referral, search, LinkedIn
- **Likely disqualifiers:** Scope/capacity mismatch; unacceptable contract; no decision path
- **Estimated commercial value:** Strategic
