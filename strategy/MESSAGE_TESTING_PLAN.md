# Message Testing Plan

**Status:** experiment specifications only. Do not publish, advertise, spend, or modify live experiences without approval. Claims/assets in a test must pass the proof policy. Randomize comparable traffic where possible; predefine exclusions; label by audience/service/geography; evaluate qualified business outcomes, not clicks alone.

**Minimum evidence default:** at least 30 qualified outcomes per variant **and** two full buying-cycle windows, unless a pre-test power calculation based on real baselines requires more. If volume cannot reach that threshold, treat results as directional and combine interviews/session review without declaring a winner. **Decision default:** adopt only if the variation improves the primary metric by at least 15% relative with no material guardrail deterioration and the confidence interval excludes no effect; otherwise continue, revise, or retain control. Thresholds are planning rules, not performance claims.

## T-01: Dust-controlled vs restoration-led

- **Hypothesis:** Dust-control lowers disruption anxiety; restoration attracts higher-intent outcome seekers.
- **Audience:** Residential refinishing.
- **Channel:** Search ad/approved landing experience.
- **Control:** Dust-controlled process headline.
- **Variation:** Assess whether restoration is feasible.
- **Primary metric:** Qualified-lead rate.
- **Guardrail metric:** Wrong-service and price-only rate; also complaint/claim-policy violations and booked gross-profit quality when available.
- **Minimum evidence required:** Default threshold above, balanced source/device/geography, validated conversion tracking, and no unresolved implementation anomaly.
- **Decision rule:** Apply the default rule within this audience only; do not generalize across segments. Stop immediately for a claims breach, deceptive interpretation, or operational overload.
