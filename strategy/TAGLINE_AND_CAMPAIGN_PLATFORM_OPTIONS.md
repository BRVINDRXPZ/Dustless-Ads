## Recommendations for testing—not publication

- **Primary brand platform:** **Clear Judgment. Controlled Process.** It best unifies homeowner confidence and B2B accountability; use as a strategic line only until tested and approved.
- **Homeowner campaign:** **Restore It Before You Replace It.** Strong decision hook, always qualified with “where feasible” and never a promise that every floor is savable.
- **Contractor campaign:** **Scope It Clearly. Finish It Carefully.** Strong on coordination without claiming contractors already call.
- **Commercial campaign:** **Residential Craftsmanship. Commercial Accountability.** Useful bridge, but commercial proof must precede external use.
- **Historic-restoration campaign:** **Restore the Character. Respect the Property.** Evokes stewardship without asserting unsupported expertise.

## Current tagline decision

“Quality You Can Stand On” remains the current repository-recorded tagline. It is memorable and approachable but generic, less local, and less explanatory of the proposed advantage. **Do not replace it automatically.** Test recognition, qualified response, multi-audience fit, and legal clearance before an owner decision. “The NOLA Floor Guys” may be a friendly campaign identity but can reduce premium/commercial perception; test it against “Hardwood Specialists,” not as an approved rename.

## Claim cautions

“The Hardwood Authority,” “The Floor Guys Contractors Call,” and similar leadership/social-proof constructions have elevated substantiation risk. Local phrases must not imply exclusive territory. Restoration lines require feasibility caveats. Trademark/domain review is required before adoption.
