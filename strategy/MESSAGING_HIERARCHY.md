# Messaging Hierarchy

**Use:** internal message system, not approved public copy. Never use “dust-free.” Validate “dust-controlled” wording before publication.

## 1–5: Brand spine

1. **Core promise:** Clear judgment, a controlled process, and hardwood work the buyer can understand and verify.
2. **Supporting messages:** restore viable floors before defaulting to replacement; hardwood specialization; clear expectations; property-aware execution; accountable coordination.
3. **Proof themes:** condition-to-outcome cases; matched before/after images; process and containment; samples and written scope; issue logs and milestones; permissioned reviews; verified credentials.
4. **Pain points:** uncertainty, dust/disruption, poor communication, mismatched repairs/color, unclear scope, trade conflicts, downtime, and fear of losing original character.
5. **Desired outcomes:** a sound decision, renewed hardwood, preserved character where feasible, predictable responsibilities, fewer surprises, and an outcome suited to use and maintenance.

## 6. Service-specific messages

| Service | Lead message | Qualification/limit |
|---|---|---|
| Refinishing | Renew worn hardwood through a condition-led sanding and finish plan. | Confirm remaining wear layer, damage, finish, occupancy, and cure rules. |
| Dust-controlled sanding | Reduce and manage airborne sanding dust with a verified system and cleanup process. | Never imply zero dust; disclose residual dust and preparation. |
| Restoration/repair | Preserve viable material and character; explain what can be blended and what may remain visible. | No invisible repair, species, or salvage promise. |
| Installation | Plan substrate, material, layout, transitions, finish, and trade dependencies as one hardwood scope. | Method/material/subfloor claims are project-specific. |
| Historic floors | Start with feasibility and preserve character where sound and practical. | “Historic specialist” and species claims need case evidence. |
| Commercial | Plan scope around access, sequencing, use, and reopening requirements. | Capability, nights/phases, deadlines, and finish suitability require verification. |
| Maintenance | Protect the finished floor with product- and use-specific care guidance. | No blanket lifespan or durability promise. |

## 7. Audience-specific messages

Use the 13 variants in `BRAND_POSITIONING.md`. Homeowners lead with clarity and care; historic owners with feasibility and character; trade partners with inputs, boundaries, and coordination; commercial buyers with risk, documents, phasing, and closeout; Northshore buyers with fit and planned travel, never blanket availability.

## 8. Calls to action

- **Universal:** “Request a floor assessment.”
- **Early qualification:** “Send photos for an initial project review.”
- **Restoration:** “Ask whether your floor may be restorable.”
- **Contractor:** “Submit plans, scope, location, and schedule.”
- **Commercial:** “Request a qualified site walkthrough.”
- **Color/finish:** “Plan your sample and finish review.”

Do not say free, instant, today, guaranteed, or priority unless approved and operationally true.

## 9. Trust-building language

“Based on the conditions we can verify”; “we will explain the tradeoffs”; “subject to site inspection”; “written scope and exclusions”; “we will flag changed conditions”; “photos help us determine the right next step”; “final color varies with species, age, sanding, light, and finish”; “manufacturer instructions govern cure and use.”

## 10. Language to avoid

Dust-free, 100% dustless, flawless, invisible repair, exact match, perfect, cheapest, best, #1, only, guaranteed color, no smell, safe/non-toxic/eco-friendly without substantiation, instant availability, any floor can be saved, historic expert without proof, commercial-ready without evidence, and attacks on competitors.

## Message assembly rule

Every external unit should contain: **buyer outcome + relevant process + one verified proof + honest limit + fitting CTA.** Before-and-after proof must show the actual same project, carry permission, and identify transformations accurately.
