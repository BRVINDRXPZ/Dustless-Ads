# Initial Domination Thesis

**Status:** Proposed Phase 2 strategy; not approved public positioning.  
**Date:** 2026-07-20

## Thesis

NOLA Dustless can become a frequently considered hardwood specialist by owning the decision moments that broad flooring retailers and generic remodelers are structurally less suited to explain: whether to restore, repair, refinish, or install; how work will affect an occupied property; how old New Orleans floors should be evaluated; and how a specialist will document and coordinate the job.

The defensible system is not a slogan. It is a compounding evidence loop:

> qualified project → disciplined documentation → permissioned proof → service/place search visibility → faster qualified response → better-fit estimate → won work and measured gross profit → stronger proof.

This is a **strategic inference**, not evidence of current superiority.

## Strategic position

### Proposed category

**Greater New Orleans hardwood restoration, refinishing, repair, and installation specialist.** Northshore coverage must be stated separately and only after owner confirmation.

### Proposed promise

**Clear judgment. Controlled process. Work you can verify.** Wording is proposed, not approved.

### Reasons to believe—only after verification

- Dust-controlled sanding process with candid limitations.
- Documented restore-versus-replace judgment.
- Permissioned before/process/after cases.
- Verified historic-floor and pine experience.
- Residential craft plus contractor/commercial operating readiness.
- Reliable response, scope, and schedule communication.

## Audience plays

### Residential

Lead with outcome and decision clarity: save character where feasible, set honest expectations, show the process, and qualify the job. Avoid discount-led positioning.

### Historic property

Build authority from documented cases, material identification, repair reasoning, and limitations. Never romanticize “historic expertise” without proof.

### Contractors/builders/designers

Sell reduced coordination risk: clear scope, bid inputs, prerequisite ownership, documentation, communication, and punch completion. The message must be backed by an operating system.

### Commercial/hospitality

Sell planning and accountability rather than generic capability: phasing, access, occupied-site controls, finish/use considerations, and reopening constraints. Pursue only owner-approved verticals and sizes.

## 90-day sequence after owner approval

### Gate 1: Truth and capacity

Resolve credentials, services, economics, minimums, geography, capacity, proof permissions, and claim language. Define what work NOLA Dustless should reject.

### Gate 2: Measurement and response

Define qualified lead and lifecycle; instrument later only with approval. Establish response ownership, hours, escalation, follow-up, and capacity pause rules.

### Gate 3: High-intent presence

Use verified evidence to specify service pages, local content, GBP improvements, review workflow, and case studies. Do not build or publish pages in Phase 2.

### Gate 4: Repeat channels

Prepare separate contractor and selected commercial acquisition systems. Use social to distribute proof and education. Consider paid search only after acceptance criteria pass.

## Dominance metrics

No targets are invented. Establish baselines and owner-approved targets for:

- qualified leads by service/geography/source;
- lead-to-estimate, estimate-to-proposal, proposal-to-win;
- booked revenue and gross profit by source/service;
- response time and contact rate;
- share of won work with source attribution;
- Maps/Search visibility for the mapped query set;
- review volume, recency, and service/location specificity;
- permissioned proof assets produced per qualified project;
- contractor/commercial opportunities, proposals, wins, and repeat work.

## Kill criteria

Pause or reject an initiative when it creates persistent wrong-service/low-value leads, exceeds response or crew capacity, cannot be attributed to qualified opportunity, requires unsupported claims, or has no credible path to gross profit. Popularity is not a reason to continue.
