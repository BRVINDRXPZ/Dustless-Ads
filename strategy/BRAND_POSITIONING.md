# Brand Positioning

**Status:** Phase 3 internal recommendation; not approved public copy. **Evidence boundary:** competitor observations remain provisional. Capability claims marked “verify” require owner evidence and approval.

## Positioning system

| Dimension | Recommended position |
|---|---|
| Category definition | A Greater New Orleans hardwood-flooring specialist for assessment, restoration, refinishing, repair, and installation; Northshore only under approved service rules. |
| Primary market position | The specialist that helps a buyer choose the right path, then executes a controlled, clearly scoped hardwood project. |
| Emotional position | Confidence that the floor’s character, the property, and the schedule will be treated with care. |
| Functional position | Condition-led recommendations, dust-controlled execution (after process verification), clear scope and communication, and documented results. |
| Local authority | New Orleans-property-aware rather than generically “Southern”; demonstrate knowledge through verified cases, never unsupported leadership claims. |
| Contractor position | A dependable hardwood subcontractor built around complete bid inputs, scope boundaries, coordination, issue documentation, and closeout. |
| Commercial position | A planning- and accountability-led hardwood partner for qualified projects where access, phasing, downtime, and reopening matter. |
| Historic-restoration position | Preserve viable original material and character where feasible; diagnose before promising. “Specialist” remains proof-gated. |

## Reasons to believe

**Repository-supported direction:** hardwood focus; preliminary refinishing, restoration, repair, installation, residential, commercial, and historic capabilities; a dust-controlled process; Greater New Orleans focus; restore-before-replace ability. These are not automatically publication-ready.

**Proof system to build:** (1) permissioned matched-angle before/process/after sets; (2) condition, recommendation, scope, limitations, and outcome case notes; (3) verified equipment and containment description; (4) scope and communication examples; (5) credentials; (6) relevant historic, contractor, and commercial cases; (7) reviews used under an approved policy.

## Evidence still required

- Exact legal name, license classification/status, insurance language, service territory, project minimums, and capacity.
- Dust-control equipment, workflow, residual-dust limitations, cleanup standard, and approved terminology.
- Permissioned project media and fact sheets; wood/species identification; historic and commercial project history.
- Contractor vendor readiness, scheduling/change-condition process, references, commercial phasing/night-work capability, and finish-system constraints.
- Verified response, scheduling, warranty, pricing, cure, occupancy, odor, durability, and maintenance language.

## Competitive alternatives

Buyers may choose another hardwood specialist, a general flooring company, retailer/installer, general contractor or handyman, replacement material, DIY, or defer work. Position against the decision risk and coordination burden—not named competitors or unverified weaknesses.

## Claims boundary

### Public claims safe now

After a final factual review, the name **NOLA Dustless Hardwood Floors**, website/contact information, and non-superlative invitations such as “Tell us about your hardwood-floor project” are low risk. Service statements and geography should remain conditional until the owner confirms current delivery boundaries.

### Public claims requiring proof

“Licensed and insured,” “dustless” as a performance claim, historic specialist, commercial experience, species/project/customer claims, years, exact territory, guarantees, schedules, pricing, durability, health/environmental benefits, and any comparative or superlative claim. “Dust-free,” “best,” “#1,” and “only” are not recommended.

## Positioning risks and controls

| Risk | Control |
|---|---|
| Name implies zero dust | Pair approved “dust-controlled” wording with candid limitations; never say dust-free. |
| Restoration promise overreaches | Say “evaluate whether restoration is feasible”; allow replacement when condition requires it. |
| Historic romance outruns proof | Gate every material/method claim to a verified case. |
| Premium becomes expensive or aloof | Explain scope, care, and reduced uncertainty; stay plainspoken and approachable. |
| Broad commercial claim strains operations | Qualify size, vertical, access, schedule, documents, and capacity before proposal. |
| One message serves everyone poorly | Use the audience variants below and separate intake paths. |
| Proposed market gap becomes a public competitor fact | Describe NOLA’s process only; never claim others lack it without current substantiation. |

## Final recommended positioning platform

**Working platform: “Clear Judgment. Controlled Process. Hardwood Work You Can Verify.”**

This is an internal strategic platform, not a published tagline. It unifies diagnosis, execution, proof, and accountability without claiming superiority. The current “Quality You Can Stand On” remains unchanged pending owner review and testing.

### What We Refuse to Be

- The cheapest flooring company or a bid won by removing necessary scope.
- A generic handyman or discount installer taking every flooring job.
- A social-media gimmick that values views over accurate proof.
- A fake luxury brand using inflated adjectives instead of useful detail.
- An unreliable subcontractor that hides schedule or condition risks.
- A company that claims everything but proves nothing.

### The Position We Should Own

**Own the role of Greater New Orleans’ proof-led hardwood decision and execution specialist: diagnose whether to restore, repair, refinish, or install; communicate the scope and limits plainly; control the process; and document the work.** “Greater New Orleans,” “specialist,” and every capability expression must pass the owner/evidence gate before external publication.
