# Objection Handling Framework

**Use:** consultative response guide, not a script for guarantees. Verify project facts, product instructions, capability, price, and schedule before responding. Escalate safety, legal, structural, environmental, contract, or health questions to qualified parties.

## Your estimate is higher

- **Recommended response:** Compare the actual scope, preparation, repair allowances, finish system, responsibilities, and exclusions—not only the total. We will explain what our scope includes and where uncertainty remains.
- **Proof needed:** Itemized scope; verified product/process; exclusions.
- **Red flags:** Buyer wants scope removed but same outcome; coercive bidding.
- **Qualification questions:** Are scopes comparable? Which items differ? What outcome matters?
- **When to walk away:** Misrepresentation, unsafe scope, abusive conduct, or price-only fit below approved economics.

## Another contractor said the floor cannot be saved

- **Recommended response:** That may be correct. We assess visible condition, remaining material, damage, structure, and goals before recommending a path; inspection may reveal replacement is appropriate.
- **Proof needed:** Assessment method; relevant cases.
- **Red flags:** Asking to contradict unseen diagnosis; structural/moisture issue ignored.
- **Qualification questions:** What was inspected and why was it judged unsavable? Any destructive tests or records?
- **When to walk away:** Unsafe/structural condition outside competence; customer requires guaranteed rescue.

## How dusty will it be

- **Recommended response:** Sanding creates dust. Our proposed language is dust-controlled, not dust-free: explain the verified collection/containment/cleanup steps and residual-dust expectations for this scope.
- **Proof needed:** Equipment/workflow; limitation and prep checklist.
- **Red flags:** Respiratory/health assurance requested; zero-dust demand.
- **Qualification questions:** Which rooms, occupants, sensitivities, HVAC, contents, and access?
- **When to walk away:** Cannot meet safety/containment needs or buyer insists on zero dust.

## Can we stay in the house

- **Recommended response:** It depends on areas, sequence, access, product instructions, ventilation, occupants, and cure restrictions. We will map those facts before advising; do not promise occupancy broadly.
- **Proof needed:** Scope plan; SDS/technical data.
- **Red flags:** Children/pets/medical issues minimized; no alternate access.
- **Qualification questions:** Who occupies, which rooms/exits, what products, and what isolation is possible?
- **When to walk away:** Occupancy would violate safety/product/site requirements.

## How long will it take

- **Recommended response:** We provide a project-specific schedule after scope and conditions are confirmed, with dependencies and cure/return-to-use distinguished from crew completion.
- **Proof needed:** Schedule template; product instructions; capacity.
- **Red flags:** Fixed deadline before inspection; other trades uncontrolled.
- **Qualification questions:** Area, condition, repairs, finish, access, event/move date, trade sequence?
- **When to walk away:** Required deadline is unsafe or impossible.

## What about the smell

- **Recommended response:** Odor varies by product, ventilation, area, and individual sensitivity. We will identify the proposed system and follow its technical/SDS guidance; no “no smell” promise.
- **Proof needed:** SDS/product data; ventilation process.
- **Red flags:** Health claim requested; undisclosed sensitivity.
- **Qualification questions:** Sensitivity, occupants, ventilation, adjacent spaces, product constraints?
- **When to walk away:** Cannot follow ventilation/safety rules.

## Will the new boards match

- **Recommended response:** We can evaluate species, grade, age, cut, dimensions, source, sanding, stain, and light. New and old wood can vary; samples and blending reduce uncertainty but do not guarantee an exact match.
- **Proof needed:** Verified repair examples; samples; sourcing records.
- **Red flags:** Demands invisible repair; species guessed from photo.
- **Qualification questions:** Existing material evidence? Size/area? Acceptable variation?
- **When to walk away:** Exact/invisible match is a condition of acceptance.

## Why does color matching vary

- **Recommended response:** Wood is a natural material. Species, age, previous treatments, sanding, grain, repairs, light, and finish all affect color. Site samples show direction, not a universal guarantee.
- **Proof needed:** Sample SOP; documented examples.
- **Red flags:** Photo/screen used as binding approval.
- **Qualification questions:** What areas/materials differ? Lighting? Desired tolerance?
- **When to walk away:** Guaranteed exact color demanded.

## Can you guarantee the stain color

- **Recommended response:** No blanket exact-color guarantee. We can document approved on-site samples and known variables, then execute the agreed system consistently.
- **Proof needed:** Signed sample process; product batch/process.
- **Red flags:** Refuses site sample; expects photo match.
- **Qualification questions:** Who approves, where will sample be, and what variation is acceptable?
- **When to walk away:** Exact outcome guarantee is mandatory.

## Why not install vinyl or laminate

- **Recommended response:** Those may be valid choices. We specialize in evaluating hardwood; compare desired character, repairability, use, substrate, maintenance, budget, and lifecycle without making unsupported product attacks.
- **Proof needed:** Accurate product/scope comparison.
- **Red flags:** False health/durability claims; buyer does not want hardwood.
- **Qualification questions:** What outcome, use, budget, timeline, and existing material matter?
- **When to walk away:** Hardwood is not desired or suitable.

## Why should I refinish instead of replace

- **Recommended response:** Refinishing may preserve viable wood and avoid unnecessary replacement, but condition and goals decide. We assess repair needs, remaining material, desired look, and scope before recommending either.
- **Proof needed:** Decision framework; cases; condition assessment.
- **Red flags:** “Any floor can be saved”; hidden structural damage.
- **Qualification questions:** What condition, history, thickness, damage, and outcome?
- **When to walk away:** Replacement is technically appropriate or customer requires certainty without inspection.

## Can you work around other trades

- **Recommended response:** Potentially, with a written sequence, site-readiness rules, access, protection, and one schedule owner. Hardwood work and cure restrictions cannot simply absorb every conflict.
- **Proof needed:** Trade prerequisite/sequence checklist.
- **Red flags:** No schedule authority; uncontrolled traffic.
- **Qualification questions:** Who controls schedule? Which trades/areas/dates? Site readiness?
- **When to walk away:** Unsafe overlaps or no responsible coordinator.

## Can you meet a commercial deadline

- **Recommended response:** We will evaluate the scope, products, access, phases, approvals, capacity, and reopening requirements before committing. A credible schedule states assumptions and decision dates.
- **Proof needed:** Comparable case; schedule/capacity/product data.
- **Red flags:** Deadline overrides cure/safety; late scope still fixed.
- **Qualification questions:** Hard deadline and reason? Decision date? Hours/access? Approval path?
- **When to walk away:** Impossible/unsafe date or unmanageable penalties/terms.

## Can you work nights or phases

- **Recommended response:** Only if owner-approved capability, site rules, crew capacity, noise/access, product restrictions, and economics support it. We will not promise from marketing copy.
- **Proof needed:** Verified night/phasing capability; plan; cases.
- **Red flags:** No security/access plan; fragmented uneconomic phases.
- **Qualification questions:** Allowed hours, phase boundaries, reopen rules, security, minimum area?
- **When to walk away:** Unsafe access, uneconomic fragmentation, or unverified capacity.

## Why should a contractor trust your crew

- **Recommended response:** Trust should come from verified documents, a clear scope, prerequisites, named communication ownership, issue documentation, schedule visibility, and references—not a slogan.
- **Proof needed:** Credentials; references; sample logs; closeout cases.
- **Red flags:** Asked to bypass contracts/docs; chronic payment issues.
- **Qualification questions:** Scope, authority, schedule, trade dependencies, vendor requirements?
- **When to walk away:** Unacceptable contract/payment/safety conditions or capacity mismatch.

## Why do you need photos or a site visit

- **Recommended response:** Photos help triage service and project fit; a visit verifies conditions that affect feasibility and scope. Neither should be represented as more conclusive than it is.
- **Proof needed:** Intake checklist; privacy policy; assessment SOP.
- **Red flags:** Refuses essential access; sends private data unnecessarily.
- **Qualification questions:** What decision is needed? Which views/measurements/history are available?
- **When to walk away:** Cannot verify conditions required for responsible scope.

## Can you do a tiny repair

- **Recommended response:** Possibly, but fit depends on location, material, damage source, sourcing, mobilization, and owner-approved minimums. Photos help us decide the right next step.
- **Proof needed:** Minimum/service rules; repair process.
- **Red flags:** Ongoing leak; unrealistic invisible match; below minimum.
- **Qualification questions:** Cause fixed? Dimensions/material? Location? Adjacent refinishing?
- **When to walk away:** Below approved minimum, wrong service, unresolved damage source.

## Can you start immediately

- **Recommended response:** We can review fit and current capacity, but we do not create false urgency or promise a start before scope, documents, materials, and site readiness are confirmed.
- **Proof needed:** Current schedule; readiness checklist.
- **Red flags:** Wants skipped assessment/prep; emergency unsafe condition.
- **Qualification questions:** Why urgent? Scope/location? Site ready? Materials/approvals?
- **When to walk away:** Required shortcuts, unsafe site, or capacity unavailable.

## Can you beat another quote

- **Recommended response:** We do not default to price matching. We can compare scope and adjust legitimate options, but will not remove necessary work or promise an equivalent result from a non-equivalent scope.
- **Proof needed:** Scope/options/economics.
- **Red flags:** Bid shopping; confidential competitor document misuse.
- **Qualification questions:** What is included/excluded? What budget/outcome is fixed?
- **When to walk away:** Cheapest-only buyer or scope falls below quality/safety/margin rules.
