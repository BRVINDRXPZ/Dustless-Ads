# Offer Architecture

**Purpose:** create valuable decision services instead of routine discounts. These are proposed intake/consultation formats—not free-service, availability, response-time, or deliverable commitments. “Launch status” means internal readiness judgment; owner approval and operating design are still required before public launch.

## Architecture principles

1. Exchange useful clarity for complete qualification inputs.
2. Match effort to potential value; photo triage precedes travel when appropriate.
3. Keep diagnosis separate from guarantees and final proposals.
4. Show inclusions, exclusions, next step, and whether fees/credits apply (TBD—owner decision).
5. Reserve high-burden walkthroughs for qualified projects.
6. Track offer → qualified lead → estimate → proposal → win → gross profit.

## Funnel and economics controls

- **Triage:** O-13 routes to decline, clarification, or a qualified assessment; it never declares final feasibility from photos.
- **Assessment:** O-01/O-04/O-10 establish facts; O-02/O-03/O-05/O-09 add specialist planning only when value supports burden.
- **B2B:** O-06/O-14 collect complete inputs before O-07/O-08; O-12 is earned, capacity-controlled, and never fake scarcity.
- **Closeout:** O-11 strengthens care and future proof without promising lifespan.
- Owner must decide paid/free/creditable treatment, outputs, scheduling, minimums, service areas, intake owner, response standard, and decline language.
