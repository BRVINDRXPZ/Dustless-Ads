# Contractor Value Proposition

> **Internal draft — owner approval required.** This is an operating specification, not authorization to contact, publish, connect a CRM, promise capacity, or make a public claim. Bracketed fields and `TBD—owner verification required` are not public copy. Use only public business contact data or permissioned records.

## Position
For contractors who need a hardwood scope handled professionally, the proposed promise is a disciplined subcontracting process: clear bid inputs, written inclusions/exclusions, schedule-awareness, field communication, documented changes and closeout—protecting the contractor’s customer relationship and reputation.
## Pillars
Reliable communication (defined owners and escalation); scope clarity and bid-readiness; hardwood specialization subject to verified service facts; historic-floor judgment only with proof; professional, customer-facing crew expectations; change-order discipline; progress/condition documentation; care and closeout readiness.
## Boundaries
Never promise perfect scheduling, flawless species/color matching, unlimited capacity, guaranteed deadlines, unknown-condition outcomes, or unapproved warranty. Say schedules depend on scope, site readiness, products, other trades and confirmed capacity.
