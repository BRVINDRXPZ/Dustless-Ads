# Risk And Compliance Policy

> **Internal draft — owner approval required.** This is an operating specification, not authorization to contact, publish, connect a CRM, promise capacity, or make a public claim. Bracketed fields and `TBD—owner verification required` are not public copy. Use only public business contact data or permissioned records.

## Non-negotiables
- This phase authorizes no contact, message, CRM connection, publication, scraping, purchase, spend or account change.
- Use public business information or permissioned first-party records; never collect private personal contact data, customer addresses or unnecessary sensitive data.
- No invented projects, clients, references, testimonials, credentials, licenses, insurance limits, bonding, union/certification status, pricing, performance, availability, capacity, deadlines or experience.
- Honor opt-outs/suppression across channels; use restrained, role-relevant one-to-one outreach only after owner approval and applicable legal/platform review. No mass spam, deceptive subject lines, automation evasion or purchased lists.
- Do not offer referral consideration without owner/legal approval and clear disclosure. Do not expose bids, client data, margins or contract records.
## Claim and document control
Every external claim maps to the proof register with source, owner, allowed channel, approval and expiry. COI/W-9/contracts and personal data live in approved secure systems, not Git. Capability statements with placeholders are never external.
## Commercial risk gates
Qualified review is required for contract, indemnity, insurance, lien/waiver, pay-if-paid, retainage, bonding, safety, labor, tax and privacy obligations. Operational approval covers scope, site readiness, schedule, phasing, occupancy, products, other trades, capacity and escalation. Missing/unsupported requirement means clarify, hold or no-bid.
## Incident response
Stop activity; preserve minimal facts; notify owner; suppress contact or withdraw claim; correct registers; obtain legal/insurance/safety advice where appropriate; document corrective action. Weekly owner audit samples claims, permissions, overdue actions and data hygiene; quarterly review retention/deletion and access.
