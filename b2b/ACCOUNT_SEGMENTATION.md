# Account Segmentation

> **Internal draft — owner approval required.** This is an operating specification, not authorization to contact, publish, connect a CRM, promise capacity, or make a public claim. Bracketed fields and `TBD—owner verification required` are not public copy. Use only public business contact data or permissioned records.

## Tier rules
| Tier | Definition | Outreach/personalization | Follow-up | Owner involvement | Proof / meeting goal | Pipeline / review | Kill criteria |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| A — Strategic recurring | recurring, high-fit and high strategic potential after research | low-volume, account plan and project-specific | tailored cadence; quarterly relationship review if engaged | owner leads key meetings, bids, escalations | segment-relevant proof; map buying process and next suitable project | account value is categorical until opportunities exist; weekly active/monthly dormant | payment, compliance, capacity or values risk; repeated price-shopping; no credible path after approved cycle |
| B — Growth | plausible mid-frequency/referral or contained commercial fit | individual role/relevance personalization | limited sequence then nurture only with relevance/permission | owner at qualification/estimate | proof matched to expressed need; fit conversation | monthly | no response after cadence, persistent mismatch, unsupported demand |
| C — Referral/network | suppliers, trades, real estate/community network | relationship/context specific; never blast | event/trigger-led; light quarterly check only if welcomed | owner approves reciprocity and conflicts | transparent referral handling; learn mutual fit | quarterly | consent issue, low-quality referrals, pay-to-play ambiguity, reputational conflict |

## Assignment
Score first; tier is a judgment, not score alone. Tier A needs recurring potential **and** operational/payment fit. Re-tier from evidence. Estimated relationship value categories: Unknown, Emerging, Growth, Strategic; never insert invented dollars. An opportunity has its own stage and value; an account without a defined project has zero opportunity pipeline value.
