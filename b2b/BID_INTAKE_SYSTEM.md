# Bid Intake System

> **Internal draft — owner approval required.** This is an operating specification, not authorization to contact, publish, connect a CRM, promise capacity, or make a public claim. Bracketed fields and `TBD—owner verification required` are not public copy. Use only public business contact data or permissioned records.

## Required intake
Project name/address; requester and decision roles; plans/specifications/addenda/finish schedule; flooring material/species/source; existing conditions; measured/estimated square feet; stairs, repairs, subfloor and moisture information; access/parking/storage/disposal; occupancy; desired schedule, bid due/start; other trades and dependencies; protection; vendor/insurance/safety requirements; contract, payment, retainage, PO, change and closeout terms.

## Workflow and checklist
- Log source/version/date; confirm whether invitation is budget, competitive, negotiated or awarded-sub scope.
- Mark each input Received / Missing / Not applicable / Needs site verification; never silently assume.
- Account fit: geography, hardwood relevance, buyer, payment/contract, capacity. Opportunity fit: scope, schedule, information, safety, proof/vendor requirements.
- Site-walk triggers: existing-floor uncertainty, repairs/stairs, occupied/phased work, moisture/subfloor concern, material transitions, commercial logistics, conflicting plans, high consequence unknown.
- Bid only if owner, scope boundary, due date, decision path, operational fit and material risks are sufficiently known. Otherwise Clarify, Defer or No-bid with reason.

## Missing-information response
“Thanks for the invitation. Before we can decide whether to bid, please provide/confirm: [items]. We have not reserved capacity. Once received, we will review fit and confirm whether a site walk or proposal is appropriate.”

## Scope-gap warning
“This review is based on [documents/version/date]. [Items] are not defined and are excluded/allowance/TBD. Conditions may require revised scope and written authorization. Schedule is not reserved until confirmed in writing.”

## Change-risk indicators
Unverified quantities/species/substrate/moisture; incomplete finish selection; occupied/furnished areas; plan/spec conflict; compressed deadline; concurrent trades; owner-supplied material; matching expectation; unknown prior sanding/finish; stairs/repairs undefined; retainage/pay-if-paid/indemnity; after-hours; evolving phasing. Escalate—do not price from guesswork.
