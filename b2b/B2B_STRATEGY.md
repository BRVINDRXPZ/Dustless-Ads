# B2B Strategy

> **Internal draft — owner approval required.** This is an operating specification, not authorization to contact, publish, connect a CRM, promise capacity, or make a public claim. Bracketed fields and `TBD—owner verification required` are not public copy. Use only public business contact data or permissioned records.

## Objective and operating thesis
Create relationship-led, proof-gated paths from a named account to qualified scope, disciplined bid, safe delivery, closeout, repeat work, referrals, revenue, and gross profit. The owner remains relationship and bid decision owner until delegation is approved. Volume never outranks fit, payment quality, capacity, or evidence.

## Six distinct motions
| Motion | Segments | Entry offer | Qualification emphasis | Account outcome |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Contractor | GCs, remodelers, builders, restoration, retailers | scope/plan review | schedule, scope, payment, repeat fit | active/repeat partner |
| Commercial | GCs, developers, facilities, multifamily | controlled walkthrough | contracts, phasing, vendor/safety requirements | qualified opportunity |
| Hospitality | hotels, restaurants | occupancy/downtime planning discussion | phasing, finish constraints, operating windows | site walk only when ready |
| Institutional | churches, institutions | stakeholder/site-condition review | approvals, access, payment, events, safety | documented buying path |
| Specifier | architects, designers, preservation professionals | constructability/finish discussion | influence, specification, proof and permissions | invited early to suitable work |
| Referral/network | realtors, trades, suppliers, manufacturers | two-way fit conversation | permission, transparency, lead fit | attributable referral relationship |

## Revenue-engine loop
1. Audit proof and vendor readiness. 2. Build only public-business/permissioned accounts. 3. Score and tier. 4. Seek warm paths first. 5. Research one relevant reason to engage. 6. Obtain owner approval before any outreach. 7. Qualify account separately from opportunity. 8. Enforce bid/no-bid. 9. Log proposal value and **assumption-labeled** margin/probability. 10. Reconcile award, invoiced revenue and gross profit from approved systems. 11. Run closeout and account review. 12. Feed documented lessons into proof, scopes and targeting.

## Capacity and governance gates
- Weekly owner capacity declaration: estimating slots, site-walk slots, crew windows, geography, scope limits, and holds; all TBD.
- No bid without buyer, location, scope, due date, start window, contract/payment requirements, and missing-information log.
- No capability statement leaves draft until every included claim has evidence and permission.
- One account owner, one next action, one date; stop on opt-out, wrong contact, no fit, or approved cadence exhaustion.
- Separate account stage from project opportunity stage; never count a relationship as pipeline revenue.
- Proposal, revenue and gross profit come only from approved records; weighted pipeline is planning math, never a forecast.

## Weekly operating review
Review capacity; overdue actions; new accounts; conversations; qualification; plans; site walks; bids; value; awards/losses; payment/contract risk; revenue/GP reconciliation; referrals; proof gaps. Decide advance, nurture, defer, no-bid, or close. Quarterly: account concentration, relationship health, repeat/referral contribution and portfolio gaps.

## Ruthless B2B priorities
### Pursue First
Warm and past relationships; suppliers able to introduce suitable work; geographically aligned residential remodelers, custom builders, restoration contractors, designers, adjacent trades, realtors and flooring retailers whose normal scopes match verified capabilities and near-term capacity. These are internal priorities, not claims that fit is proven.

### Build Proof For
Historic-preservation professionals, larger builders, property managers, multifamily, restaurants, churches, hotels, commercial GCs, developers, facilities teams and insurance restoration: pursue only after relevant case evidence, references/permissions, vendor documents, contract controls and operational answers exist.

### Hold Until Operationally Ready
Occupied/phased hospitality, large multifamily, institutional procurement, high-compliance facilities, slow-pay/retainage work, after-hours demands, bonding requirements, broad geographic programs, or simultaneous projects exceeding declared capacity.

### Do Not Chase
Low-value mass outreach; unqualified bid invitations; work outside profitable geography; unsupported qualifications; unacceptable payment risk; capacity-damaging projects; vague scopes/unrealistic deadlines; repeat price-shopping; misleading-claim relationships; or commercial expansion without documentation and controls.
