# Content Requirements

## Approval rule

Every factual claim must be classified as verified, owner-approved, proposed, or placeholder. Only verified and approved copy may migrate to production. Current prototype language describes decision processes and questions; it does not assert credentials, project counts, reviews, guarantees, leadership, dust elimination, price, or unlimited coverage.

## Per-page content acceptance

Each service page needs: approved headline/location; exact service scope; operationally deliverable process; candid exclusions; qualification rules; 4–6 owner-approved FAQs; service-area facts; CTA/offer; approved phone/hours/address policy; privacy review; and matched, permissioned proof.

Shared proof requires asset ID, project/service, capture date, truthful caption, location display permission, customer permission, allowed channels, expiration/revocation notes, and approver. Testimonials require exact approved text, attribution rule, source, date, permission, and no selective alteration.

## Editorial checks

Use “dust-controlled,” never “dust-free.” Qualify restoration with feasibility. Do not promise invisible matching. Do not call the business a historic specialist, market leader, licensed, insured, commercial-ready, or available in a town until corresponding evidence and approval are registered.
