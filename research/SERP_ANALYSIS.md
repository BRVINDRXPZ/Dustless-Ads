# Phase 2 SERP Analysis

**Research date:** 2026-07-20  
**Status:** Completed strategic SERP framework; live rank, local-pack, review, and ad-auction observations remain **unverified** because internet/search access was unavailable in this execution environment.

## Evidence labels

- **Verified fact:** supported by repository instructions or a directly inspectable first-party source.
- **Public observation:** visible on a public page or search surface during an accessible research pass.
- **Strategic inference:** a recommended conclusion derived from observed structure, buyer intent, or known channel mechanics—not a fact about a competitor.
- **Unverified claim:** a public/company statement that has not been substantiated independently.
- **Owner confirmation required:** internal capability, economics, permission, or boundary only the owner can validate.

## Method and limitations

The requested query set was converted into a reproducible intent map. A 35-entity market set was assembled: 20 direct hardwood competitors, 10 broader flooring businesses, and 5 adjacent restoration/remodeling organizations. National discovery surfaces and franchises were also mapped. The environment returned authorization/network errors for live search, websites, Maps, ad auctions, and social pages. Consequently:

1. No current rank position, map-pack appearance, review count/rating/recency, ad impression, or posting cadence is presented as fact.
2. Candidate names and URLs in `COMPETITOR_MATRIX.csv` are research leads, not endorsements or proof of current operation/service coverage.
3. “Not observed” never means “does not exist.” It means the evidence was not accessible in this pass.
4. Before publishing comparisons or allocating money, rerun the observation protocol below from a neutral, owner-approved browser and archive dated screenshots/URLs.

## Reproducible live-observation protocol

For each query and geography:

1. Use a signed-out/incognito desktop browser and a mobile device; record date, time, device, and explicit location.
2. Capture paid placements, local pack, organic top 10, People Also Ask, directories, images, and video modules.
3. Record each business only as displayed: name, position, URL, rating, count, snippet, ad label, and destination page.
4. Repeat from New Orleans, Metairie, and one Northshore location. Location materially changes “near me” and Maps results.
5. Run one repeat seven days later to distinguish durable visibility from a transient result.
6. Never infer ad spend or performance from one impression. Mark only “ad observed for query/date/location.”
7. Archive screenshots internally; do not republish competitor assets.

## Strategic findings

### 1. Google Search and Maps opportunity

**Strategic inference:** win a service × place architecture rather than one generic “flooring” page. The first cluster should be refinishing, dust-controlled sanding, restoration, repair, installation, and commercial/contractor capability, supported by authentic project evidence and only genuinely distinct location content. Google Business Profile should mirror those verified services, publish recent permissioned project media, and solicit compliant reviews that naturally describe service and location.

Do not mass-produce thin neighborhood doorway pages. A location page earns its place only with distinct logistics, local proof, relevant housing/floor conditions that can be supported, and a useful conversion path.

### 2. High-intent demand before broad awareness

**Verified fact:** the repository directive prioritizes high-intent demand. Therefore refinishing/restoration/installation/repair queries and local discovery should precede broad social reach or display activity.

### 3. Specialist advantage versus retailers

**Strategic inference:** retailers can dominate generic “hardwood flooring” through selection and brand scale. NOLA Dustless should avoid trying to out-assort them. It can instead own diagnosis and execution: restore versus replace, repair feasibility, sanding/finish process, historic-material judgment, and an accountable specialist crew—once evidence supports those claims.

### 4. Northshore gap

**Strategic inference:** the Northshore deserves a separate cluster and GBP/service-area validation, not a buried sentence. **Owner confirmation required:** exact towns, travel threshold, minimum scope, scheduling model, and proof from completed Northshore work.

### 5. B2B gap

**Strategic inference:** “flooring contractor” results often mix retailers, residential installers, directories, and broad commercial flooring firms. A procurement-ready capability page could differentiate with scopes, service territory, bid intake, documentation readiness, schedule communication, occupied-site planning, and project types. Every credential and capacity statement requires owner confirmation.
