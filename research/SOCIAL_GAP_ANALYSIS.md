## Best social-media gap

**Strategic inference:** become the local hardwood “proof library,” not merely another transformation feed. Every qualified project should produce a consistent evidence bundle:

1. Conditions and customer objective (without private details).
2. Floor/species identification only when verified.
3. Repair/restoration decision and why.
4. Containment/sanding/finish process with honest limitations.
5. Before, key process moments, and matched-angle after.
6. Maintenance guidance.
7. Permission and claim record.

This format creates reusable assets for Search, Maps, estimates, social, and contractor credibility.

## Cadence proposal—not a claim about current capacity

- 2 short vertical videos/week.
- 1 project carousel/week.
- 1 educational post/week.
- 1 longer YouTube case study/month.
- 1 LinkedIn B2B/process post/week.
- Stories only when there is meaningful, permissioned progress.

Owner must approve capture burden, spokesperson, editing capacity, and publishing workflow.

## Engagement-quality rubric

Do not rank competitors by followers. Score a 20-post sample on:

- relevance of comments/questions;
- local audience signals;
- saves/shares where visible;
- evidence specificity;
- visual consistency;
- response quality;
- path to inquiry;
- frequency of unsupported claims.

## Immediate non-publishing actions

1. Create an asset permission register.
2. Define a shot list that crews can execute in under ten minutes per milestone.
3. Inventory existing vertical video, matched before/after pairs, testimonials, and B2B proof.
4. Draft factual captions only after species, method, location, and permission are confirmed.
5. Create platform account/access inventory without storing credentials.
