# Owner Research Instructions

**Purpose:** collect dated public evidence without contacting a company, buying anything, signing into a competitor account, or treating one result as permanent. These instructions gather observations only; they do not authorize publishing comparisons.

## Before each session

1. Create an evidence folder named `research/evidence/YYYY-MM-DD/` (or send the files to Codex in one dated folder).
2. Use a normal internet connection in the city being recorded. Do not use a VPN unless its location is recorded.
3. Open a new incognito/private browser window, stay signed out of Google, and turn off ad blockers. Record browser, desktop/mobile, city, date, local time, and whether location permission is on.
4. Open the relevant CSV template in a spreadsheet. Never overwrite an earlier observation; add a row.

## 1. Google searches and organic rankings

1. Copy a query exactly from `SEARCH_INTENT_MAP.csv`; do not add a company name.
2. Search once from the stated city on desktop and once on mobile. Avoid clicking results before the screenshot.
3. In `SERP_CAPTURE_TEMPLATE.csv`, create one row for every organic result in the first 10 organic positions. Count only organic results; keep map, ad, directory, image, and video sections labeled separately.
4. Record the displayed position, company/name, title, URL, and snippet exactly as displayed. A directory result is the directory—not every business on its page.
5. Capture the full relevant result area. Repeat the observation seven days later before calling visibility recurring.

## 2. Google Maps results

1. From the same query, capture the local pack on the results page, then open Maps and run the query again.
2. Do not use “search this area” after moving the map. Record the actual city/location context and whether precise location was enabled.
3. Enter displayed order, name, category, profile URL, service-area/address text, rating, review count, hours/status, and any sponsored label in `GOOGLE_MAPS_CAPTURE_TEMPLATE.csv`.
4. Screenshot the full pack/list with the query visible. A position is only an observation for that query, location, device, and time—not a permanent rank.

## 3. Sponsored search results

1. Record a result as paid only when Google visibly labels it **Sponsored**, **Ad**, or an equivalent paid label.
2. Complete `PAID_SEARCH_OBSERVATION_TEMPLATE.csv`, including advertiser, domain, position, headline/description themes, extensions, and landing-page URL.
3. Capture the label in the screenshot. If no ad appears, record “none observed” for that exact context; never conclude the company is not advertising or infer budget/performance.

## 4. Reviews

1. Open the exact Google Maps, Yelp, Facebook, or BBB profile. Do not combine platforms.
2. Record the displayed rating, review count, most recent review date, number sampled, recurring service/location themes, and owner-response activity in `REVIEW_AUDIT_TEMPLATE.csv`.
3. Screenshot the profile header and the newest-review sort/filter. Do not copy personal reviewer information into this repository. Paraphrase themes; use only a very short quotation when essential.

## 5. Websites, services, offers, and positioning

1. Start with the candidate URL in `COMPETITOR_VALIDATION_QUEUE.csv`, but confirm the company identity on the page.
2. Capture the homepage, relevant service page, service-area/contact page, and any offer or commercial/contractor/historic page. Record the exact page URL and date.
3. Distinguish what the company **claims** from what independent evidence verifies. A company website supports “the company states X,” not necessarily “X is true.”
4. Never submit forms, start chats, call, request quotes, accept cookies unnecessarily, or download gated material.

## 6. Social activity

For Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, YouTube, and LinkedIn, locate profiles from the company website or a clearly matching identity. In `SOCIAL_AUDIT_TEMPLATE.csv`, record profile URL, active/not found, displayed counts, newest post date, posts sampled, observed cadence, content themes, CTAs, positioning, and visible engagement fields. Capture the profile header and enough recent posts to support the sample. “Not found” means not found in this pass—not that no profile exists. Do not follow, like, comment, message, or engage.

## 7. Required context and screenshot names

Every row needs date, local time, exact query, city/location, device, browser/context, screenshot filename, and verifier initials. Use lowercase words separated by hyphens in this convention:

`YYYY-MM-DD_platform_query_company_sequence.png`

Example: `2026-07-20_google-serp_hardwood-floor-refinishing-new-orleans_nola-hardwood-floors_01.png`. Remove customer names, notifications, email addresses, tabs, and other private data before saving.

## 8. Return the evidence

Preferred method: add screenshots under `research/evidence/YYYY-MM-DD/`, add completed rows to the matching templates, and give each supported claim a unique row in `EVIDENCE_REGISTER.csv`. If repository upload is inconvenient, provide Codex one dated folder plus the completed CSV files. Do not send passwords, tokens, private customer data, or account exports containing personal information.

Before handoff, confirm that every CSV screenshot name exists, every source URL opens, blanks are explained in Notes, and observation language does not claim permanence. Codex should then cross-link Evidence IDs, update validation statuses, and preserve conflicting observations rather than silently replacing them.
